Intro to Leonardo

Tweet
Thu 27 March 2014
By William Van Hevelingen

In graphite.

tags: graphiteleonardometrics

Leonardo is a Graphite dasboard written by Julien Dehee (PrFalken). It's based on and closely resembles Gdash. Both Gdash and Leonardo allow the user to specify the construction of graphs through configuration files. Gdash implents its own dsl and Leonardo uses yaml files. This allows you to easily script and automate the construction of dashboards with bash or your favorite configuration management software.

I choose to go with Leonardo over Gdash mostly due to lazyness. I was already familar with yaml and it seemed easier to template in Puppet than Gdash's dsl. Additionally, Leonardo shipped with better documentation and when I asked for better examples PrFalken responded within a few hours with examples and suggestions on how to debug my graphs not rendering.

Installing Leonardo is pretty easy. The installation instructions are availible on the README. For Puppet users you can use the Puppet module I wrote for it.
